Programming tools for data journalism

I was recently awarded funding from Google Digital News Initiative to work on TheGamma. TheGamma is available on GitHub and npm. You can see it in action in three sample articles:

  • Accounting for democracy looks at UK government spending
  • Expected and unexpected lets you guess market trends
  • Visualising Olympic medalists looks at the history of Olympics

The work on pivot type provider appeared in ECOOP 2017 and a recent draft looks at the live programming aspects of TheGamma.

Context-aware programming languages

In my PhD thesis, I worked on theory of context-aware programming languages also known as coeffects. The best way to learn about coeffects is to read my interactive essay which explains the concepts and lets you run simple programs interactively. For academic references, please refer to our ICFP 2014 and ICALP 2013 papers.

Supervisions and teaching

I'm still based in Cambridge and I'm happy to supervise Part II (final year) projects that are related to data science, visualization, programming languages programming, F# (or other functional languages), JavaScript and web programming or other interesting topics. If you're at Cambridge and want to discuss your idea, get in touch! I also supervised a few Charles University students every now and then.

To get an idea about projects, have a look at the work of some great Part II students who I had the pleasure of supervising:

  • Eduardo Munoz, working on Type-safe multilanguage programming
  • Lewis Brown's Refactoring tool for F#, also at GSoC 2013
  • David Barker, working on Functional Reactive Programming for Data Binding in C# (Bitbucket)

I also supervised a number of Computer Laboratory courses for various colleges and I'm happy to supervise them again. The courses include the obvious PL-related suspects including Semantics of Programming Languages, Concepts in Programming Language, Types, Optimising Compilers and Denotational Semantics.

Academic community

I am a co-chair and co-organiser of Salon des Refusés 2017, and Salon des Refusés 2018, a workshop series at ‹Programming› that provided a venue for presenting and discussing new ideas and new ways of doing computer science.

I was a member of the Program Committee (PC) for IFL 2018, OBT 2017, TyDe 2016, HaPoP 3, TFP 2015, ML 2013, ML 2012, CUFP 2012 and PSW 2012 and a member of External Review Committee (XRC) for ISMM 2012 and ISMM 2011. I also reviewed papers for P & T, JLAMP, PLACES 2016, CiE 2015, PPDP 2015, PPDP 2013, TLCA 2013, CC 2013 and ECOOP 2012.

I'm happy to review papers that (broadly) match my interest in data-driven programming, programming models, types, concurrent and reactive programming, web and language integration, philosophy of programming (and also any controversial, provocative and novel work).

Teaching in Prague

As an undergraduate and Master's student at the Charles University in Prague, I helped to organize and teach non-compulsory courses.

Programming languages F# and OCaml (English)

Charles University, Winter 2009/2010

The lecture teaches programming in the ML-family of languages and highlights the mathematical foundations of ML languages. As a results students should find it easier to understand many mathematical concepts. It also explains advanced F# features such as computation expressions.

WinFX Programming (Czech)

Charles University, Summer 2005/2006

A practically oriented seminar about Windows programming using the WinFX framework (now called WPF, WCF and WF), which was in beta version in 2006. It discussed graphics, working with data and communication between applications (Jointly taught with Vojtech Jakl).
